## 3. Root Cause Analysis: Why Previous Candidate Searches Stagnated

In Phase 23.1, deep reconciliation revealed that generating thousands of candidate parameter sets did not produce meaningful trading diversity. Phase 24 diagnosed the structural cause within `src/strategies/candidates.py`:

1.  **Unwired Signal Parameters:** Parameters such as `adx_thresh`, `rsi_overbought`, `rsi_oversold`, `wick_ratio_thresh`, `volume_trend_thresh`, and `funding_threshold` were stored in strategy dictionaries during instantiation but were ignored inside the core `get_signal()` execution loop for several template types (e.g., `bollinger_expansion_breakout`).
2.  **Default Overrides:** When strategies evaluated breakout or mean-reversion conditions, hardcoded numerical thresholds were used instead of dynamically referencing `self.params`. Consequently, altering a candidate's RSI threshold from 70 to 85 or ADX from 20 to 30 produced identical trade logs.
3.  **Hash Deduplication Collapse:** Because trade logs were identical across disparate parameter sets, the behavioral deduplication engine correctly flagged them as duplicates, collapsing apparent candidate counts from thousands down to a trivial handful of unique equity curves.
